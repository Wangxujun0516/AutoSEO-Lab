B2B Industrial Procurement Guide: CE/OSHA Compliance & Supplier Verification ​

Target Audience: OEM Procurement Managers | Industry: RV / Industrial Equipment | Skill Level: Advanced

📋 Executive Summary ​

For B2B industrial buyers sourcing from Chinese manufacturers, navigating the compliance landscape requires a systematic approach. This guide provides a deterministic framework for verifying supplier credentials, understanding CE/OSHA documentation requirements, and conducting due diligence on industrial component suppliers.

1. Supplier Verification Framework ​

1.1 Intertek Verified Supplier Status ​

What is Intertek Verified?

Intertek Verified is a supplier verification program that confirms:

Verification CriteriaDescription
Legal ExistenceBusiness license registration and valid tax ID
Physical AddressFactory/office location confirmation via on-site visit
Production CapacityEquipment inventory, workforce, and manufacturing processes
Quality ManagementISO 9001:2015 certification and quality control procedures
Export HistoryPast export records and customer references

Verification Process:

text
Supplier Application → Document Submission → On-Site Audit → 
Verification Report → Annual Re-certification

For Buyers: Request the Intertek Verified certificate number and verify at Intertek Supplier Verification Portal.

2. CE Certification Requirements ​

2.1 CE Marking Overview ​

The CE marking indicates compliance with EU health, safety, and environmental protection standards. For industrial equipment, the relevant directives are:

DirectiveScope
2006/42/ECMachinery Directive
2014/30/EUElectromagnetic Compatibility (EMC)
2014/35/EULow Voltage Directive (LVD)
2015/863/EURoHS (Restriction of Hazardous Substances)

2.2 Technical Documentation Requirements ​

According to the Machinery Directive (2006/42/EC), the technical file must include:

DocumentDescription
Risk AssessmentSystematic analysis of hazards and risks
Technical SpecificationsDrawings, diagrams, component lists
Test ReportsEMC, LVD, and performance test results
User ManualInstallation, operation, maintenance instructions
Declaration of ConformitySigned by the manufacturer or authorized representative

2.3 EU Authorized Representative ​

For non-EU manufacturers:

  • Must appoint an EU-based Authorized Representative
  • The representative must be identified on the product labeling
  • The representative's contact details must be included in the technical documentation

3. OSHA Compliance for Industrial Equipment ​

3.1 OSHA General Industry Standards ​

StandardTitleKey Requirements
29 CFR 1910.132Personal Protective EquipmentPPE requirements for workers
29 CFR 1910.133Eye and Face ProtectionSafety glasses, face shields
29 CFR 1910.147Lockout/TagoutEnergy control procedures
29 CFR 1910.212Machine GuardingGuarding requirements for machinery
29 CFR 1910.217Mechanical Power PressesSafety standards for presses
29 CFR 1910.303Electrical InstallationsWiring methods and materials

3.2 OSHA Machine Guarding Requirements ​

General Requirements:

  • Point-of-operation guards must prevent operator access to hazardous areas
  • Guards must be attached to the machine or secured in place
  • Guards must not create additional hazards
  • Interlocking devices must prevent machine operation when guards are removed

Power Transmission Guarding:

  • All belts, pulleys, gears, sprockets, and chains must be guarded
  • Guards must be of substantial construction
  • Guard openings must be small enough to prevent hand/finger insertion

4. Supplier Due Diligence Checklist ​

4.1 Pre-Qualification Checklist ​

  • [ ] Business license and tax registration certificate
  • [ ] ISO 9001:2015 certification
  • [ ] CE certification (for EU market)
  • [ ] Intertek Verified Supplier status
  • [ ] Factory location and facilities photos
  • [ ] Production capacity and lead time
  • [ ] Minimum order quantity (MOQ)
  • [ ] Payment terms and conditions
  • [ ] Warranty policy
  • [ ] Shipping and logistics capabilities

4.2 Quality Audit Checklist ​

  • [ ] Incoming material inspection process
  • [ ] In-process quality control
  • [ ] Final inspection and testing procedures
  • [ ] Calibration records for measuring equipment
  • [ ] Non-conformity handling process
  • [ ] Corrective and preventive action (CAPA) system

  • [ ] Traceability system for components
  • [ ] Environmental management system (ISO 14001)
  • [ ] Social compliance (SA8000, BSCI)

  • [ ] Warehouse and inventory management
  • [ ] Quality control laboratory

6. Procurement Decision Framework ​

6.1 Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Model ​

TCO = Purchase Price + Logistics + Installation + Maintenance + Downtime + Disposal

Key TCO Components:

ComponentDescriptionWeight
Purchase PriceUnit cost from supplier30%
LogisticsShipping, customs, duties15%
InstallationLabor and integration costs10%
MaintenanceRoutine service and repairs20%
DowntimeProduction loss due to failures20%

7. Request for Quotation (RFQ) Template ​

7.1 RFQ Structure ​

  1. Company Information

    • Company name and contact details
    • Authorized signatory

  2. Product Specifications

    • Product description and technical requirements
    • Quantity and delivery schedule
    • Acceptance criteria

  3. Quality Requirements

    • Certifications required
    • Testing and inspection procedures
    • Quality control standards

  4. Commercial Terms

    • Price and payment terms
    • Delivery terms (Incoterms)
    • Warranty and liability

  5. Documentation Requirements

    • Technical documentation
    • Certificates and test reports
    • Packing and labeling requirements

  6. Evaluation Criteria

    • Scoring methodology
    • Weighting factors

  7. Timeline

    • RFQ issuance date
    • Deadline for responses
    • Expected award date

7.2 Sample RFQ Checklist ​

  • [ ] Product specifications with dimensional drawings
